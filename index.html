Tips or Gratuity for Gigs 9 months ago Updated Gigs on the Gigpro platform are considered flat rate, meaning that the rate per hour for the Gig is all the Pro should expect to be paid. If a Gig is posted for $20/hr. then the Pro should expect to be paid $20/hr. for the Gig less the OAI (Occupational Accident Insurance) fee and that is all. If a Gig description includes the words "plus tips" or "plus gratuity", then the business will be expected to pay the Pro tips in cash at the end of the shift. If the Gig description does not include these terms, then the Pro should not expect to be paid tips for the Gig. Occupational Accident Insurance protects the Pro and Business by serving as the injury policy of record in the event a Pro is injured during a shift. What is OAI? Occupational Accident Insurance, or OAI, is an alternative solution to Workers Compensation. Workers Compensation is for W-2 employees only and does not apply to 1099 Independent Contractors coming in to pick up a shift. Workers Compensation is an employer sponsored policy and responsibility. OAI is an Independent Contractor policy and responsibility that is sponsored by an associated company (Gigpro). The cost of the coverage for each shift ($0.38/hour) is withheld from the Pro’s payment and paid on their behalf. OAI is required for all Pros on all shifts on Gigpro, with no exceptions being made. recent blog post OAI: https://www.gigpro.com/blog/whats-the-difference-between-occupational-accident-insurance-and-workers-comp/ OAI does not cover: Drivers, Gas powered machinery (backpack blowers or lawn equipment), Heavy machinery (forklifts), Automated machinery (bottlers), Ladders, or Security Gigs where touching another person or their belongings is required. ID & Ticket scanning is allowed. For even more information see our recent blog post here. If you have any questions about the OAI policy and it’s coverage, please click here to fill out our business support form and we will be glad to assist you.
